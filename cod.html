Call Of Duty 1



Call of Duty 1 marks the beginning of the call of duty franchise. The game gives us a good experience but today it’s a little outdated. The graphics are old and not as great as the games today but if you are new and want to start an action gaming series, this game is perfect. Though outdated the sound effects of the game makes it way better and thrilling but you will not find it as thrilling in the consoles at is in the PC. The campaign is quite short but its good and fighting against the Nazis add more fun. The multiplayer mode has 6 modes and works perfectly for an old game. COD 1 really focused on the AI controlled NPC's, separating it from other games. The scale and grandeur of the environment textures really differentiated it and made the game a great start for the franchise. If you are really interested in the story modes, you should start from here.


Call Of Duty 2



Call of duty is everything one would expect from a sequel to the first game. One aspect that has changes you do not have health bars anymore instead the screen will start growing red more and more as you take damage, and the character starts panting. You can take cover to regenerate the health and as the screen clears your health is fully regained. Call of Duty 2 introduced us to the smoke grenades. You can just drop the smoke grenade and neutralize the machine gunners and fool the snipers. The enemy AI has improved a lot they will through grenades and keep on shooting until you die. The game reload after death is fast and not a lot of progress is lost as the game auto saves a lot. The environment textures have improved a little from COD 1. The story mode is orchestrated in such a manner that you will not care for the graphics or the sounds. Multiplayer Call of Duty 2 offers standard deathmatch, team deathmatch, and capture-the-flag modes, along with the search-and-destroy mode from the original game. A mode called "headquarters" is also available, and it's probably the most enjoyable mode of the five available in COD 2. If you liked the original and have been thirsting for more, Call of Duty 2 will deliver that.






Call Of Duty 3



Call of Duty 3 gameplay is like that of Call of Duty 2 but there are a few changes in the combat. Players fight alongside AI-controlled teammates against enemies who use various attack patterns, hide behind cover, and regroup to improved defensive positions. The developers have introduced us to the throwing back grenade, the player can now catch and throw the grenades back at the enemy, though a tricky technique it is quite fun. Also new are action events that have replaced mundane tasks like placing a charge or aiming a mortar. COD 3 does a good job of humanizing the soldiers reminding us momentarily that real heroes don't have to resemble a AI controlled robot. The bushes and trees now sway with the wind thus improving the environment textures with every new game in the series. There are many jaw dropping moments and combining them with the lighting effect and the music the game reach new heights. New character classes and automobiles have been introduced in the online wars making it more intelligent and thrilling. Call of Duty 3 is a good game and worth playing.




Call Of Duty 4: Modern Warfare



Call of Duty 4 is more thrilling , fun, inetense and gripping than the previous call of duty games. The enemies are more smarter and the weapons are more deadlier. You will eqyip the weapons of the AC-130 Gunship, flying above the battlefield and fires 105mm rounds that decimate the enemy. Throughout the campaign you will hop between playable charactes one of the British S.A.S and U.S Marines. The combat has a big chance in COD4. The shooting is intense as the number of enemy increases. Though a linear shooter that essentially funnels you through corridors, there are a considerable number of side paths. These almost always lead to flanking positions. If you do not flank the enemy, rest assured they will flank you. The enemy has the greatest weapon the dogs, yes dogs they are quite challenging and makes us furious a lot. The sound and texture makes the game fun and more evolved from the first COD. COD4 multiplayer is similar to the previous one but few things have been removed and few have been introduced. COD4 will shake loose your fillings and have you begging for more.




Call Of Duty: World at War

Call of Duty: World at war takes us back to the world war 2 era. It focuses on the less popularized parts of the world war. The game is not only rigidly scripted but also fast and fluid. The campaign mode suffers in this game beacuse the narrator tends to skip months at a time.
